# Week 19-20 Reading List

## Essential Papers

### Multimodal AI

1. **CLIP: Learning Transferable Visual Models From Natural Language Supervision**
   - Authors: Alec Radford et al. (OpenAI)
   - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.00020
   - Key concepts: Contrastive learning, vision-language alignment, zero-shot transfer

2. **An Image is Worth 16x16 Words: Transformers for Image Recognition at Scale (ViT)**
   - Authors: Alexey Dosovitskiy et al. (Google)
   - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.11929
   - Key concepts: Vision transformers, patch embeddings, attention for images

3. **Robust Speech Recognition via Large-Scale Weak Supervision (Whisper)**
   - Authors: Alec Radford et al. (OpenAI)
   - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.04356
   - Key concepts: Large-scale audio training, multitask learning, robustness

4. **Flamingo: a Visual Language Model for Few-Shot Learning**
   - Authors: Jean-Baptiste Alayrac et al. (DeepMind)
   - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.14198
   - Key concepts: Vision-language interleaving, few-shot multimodal learning

5. **BLIP: Bootstrapping Language-Image Pre-training**
   - Authors: Junnan Li et al.
   - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.12086
   - Key concepts: Captioning, VQA, image-text retrieval

### Responsible AI

6. **Attention is Not Explanation**
   - Authors: Sarthak Jain and Byron C. Wallace
   - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.10186
   - Key concepts: Model interpretability, attention mechanisms, explainability

7. **Model Cards for Model Reporting**
   - Authors: Margaret Mitchell et al. (Google)
   - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.03993
   - Key concepts: Transparency, documentation, ethical AI deployment

8. **Fairness and Abstraction in Sociotechnical Systems**
   - Authors: Andrew Selbst et al.
   - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.03265
   - Key concepts: Fairness metrics, sociotechnical systems, abstraction traps

9. **On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots**
   - Authors: Emily M. Bender et al.
   - Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3442188.3445922
   - Key concepts: Language model risks, environmental impact, bias

10. **Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback**
    - Authors: Anthropic
    - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073
    - Key concepts: AI alignment, self-critique, harmlessness training

### RLHF

11. **Training Language Models to Follow Instructions with Human Feedback**
    - Authors: Long Ouyang et al. (OpenAI)
    - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.02155
    - Key concepts: RLHF, instruction following, reward modeling

12. **Fine-Tuning Language Models from Human Preferences**
    - Authors: Daniel Ziegler et al. (OpenAI)
    - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1909.08593
    - Key concepts: Preference learning, human feedback, reward models

### Bias and Fairness

13. **Man is to Computer Programmer as Woman is to Homemaker? Debiasing Word Embeddings**
    - Authors: Tolga Bolukbasi et al.
    - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1607.06520
    - Key concepts: Word embedding bias, gender bias, debiasing methods

14. **Fairness and Machine Learning: Limitations and Opportunities**
    - Authors: Solon Barocas, Moritz Hardt, and Arvind Narayanan
    - Link: https://fairmlbook.org/
    - Key concepts: Comprehensive fairness guide, mathematical foundations

15. **Gender Shades: Intersectional Accuracy Disparities in Commercial Gender Classification**
    - Authors: Joy Buolamwini and Timnit Gebru
    - Link: http://proceedings.mlr.press/v81/buolamwini18a.html
    - Key concepts: Intersectional bias, commercial AI systems, evaluation
